Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Founders Agreement?
A Founders Agreement is a legally binding document between co-founders of a startup that outlines equity ownership, roles, responsibilities, IP assignments, vesting schedules, and exit terms to prevent future disputes.
When should founders sign this agreement?
Ideally, before the company is incorporated or immediately after. Early execution prevents future misunderstandings about equity and responsibilities as the startup grows.
Is a Founders Agreement legally enforceable in India?
Yes. When drafted correctly and signed on appropriate stamp paper, a Founders Agreement is legally enforceable under the Indian Contract Act, 1872.
Does it differ from a Shareholders Agreement?
Yes. A Founders Agreement is for the early pre-investor stage, focusing on internal co-founder relationships. A Shareholders Agreement typically comes later when investors are involved.
